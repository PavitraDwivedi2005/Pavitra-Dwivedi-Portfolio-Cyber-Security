the Code

What drives me, where I've been, and where I'm heading.

Who I Am

I'm Pavitra Dwivedi, a Computer Science undergraduate at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies, Dehradun. My focus is on building backend systems that are robust, scalable, and secure. I work primarily with Python, Java, Django, and REST APIs, with a growing specialisation in cybersecurity and privacy-preserving AI.

What drives me isn't just writing code — it's solving real-world problems. Whether it's designing a scam detection system that never stores voice data, or building ransomware-resilient backup infrastructure, I believe technology should protect and empower users.

My Journey

My fascination with technology started with curiosity about how systems break. That curiosity led me through two internships that shaped my technical direction:

At INFERA, Noida, I conducted VAPT on client web infrastructure, identifying 15+ vulnerabilities including OWASP Top-10 issues, analysed SIEM logs during simulated incidents, and developed 6+ SOC runbooks and incident response playbooks.

At IQSec Labs, I built a data anonymisation pipeline in Python that preserved 85% data utility while anonymising 80% of sensitive fields. I also automated preprocessing with Python and Bash, cutting manual processing by 60-70%, and contributed to a Foreign Object Detection system with 90%+ accuracy.

I also co-authored a peer-reviewed research paper on cybersecurity and digital forensics, presented at an international conference and published in proceedings (ISBN: 978-93-342-1179-5).

What I'm Building Now

Currently working on a Privacy-Preserving Real-Time Scam Detection System — a pipeline that analyses voice calls in real-time using transient in-memory audio processing and explainable AI risk scoring. The system never stores raw voice data. Copyright filing is in progress for the architecture and methodology.

I'm also deepening my skills in Kubernetes, cloud security, and federated learning — working toward building distributed, privacy-first systems at scale.

Vision

I aspire to work at the intersection of security engineering and backend infrastructure. My goal is to join a team building secure systems that serve millions. Long-term, I want to contribute to open-source security tooling and continue publishing research at the intersection of AI and cybersecurity.
