What drives me, where I've been, and where I'm heading.

Who I Am

I'm Pavitra Dwivedi, a Computer Science undergraduate at the University of Petroleum and Energy Studies, Dehradun. My focus is on Security and Site Reliability Engineering (SRE). I work primarily with Python, Bash, and Java, with a growing specialisation in building secure, reliable, and scalable systems.

What drives me isn't just writing code — it's solving real-world problems. Whether it's designing a scam detection system that never stores voice data, building ransomware-resilient backup infrastructure, or developing observability pipelines, I believe technology should protect and empower users.

My Journey

My fascination with technology started with curiosity about how systems break and how to keep them running. That curiosity led me through two internships that shaped my technical direction:

At INFERA, Noida, as a Cybersecurity Intern, I conducted VAPT assessments on web applications, identifying 15+ vulnerabilities including OWASP Top 10 issues, monitored and analyzed SIEM logs to detect anomalies, and developed SOC runbooks and incident response workflows.

At IQSec Labs, as a Security & Data Engineering Intern, I built and maintained data pipelines for sensitive datasets, automated data processing reducing manual effort by 60-70%, and improved system reliability by optimizing preprocessing workflows.

I also co-authored a peer-reviewed research paper on cybersecurity and digital forensics, presented at an international conference and published in proceedings (ISBN: 978-93-342-1179-5).

What I'm Building Now

Currently, I'm working on several projects focused on security and observability. I'm building a Privacy-Preserving Real-Time Scam Detection System that analyses voice calls in real-time without storing raw data, with a copyright filing in progress.

I'm also building an API Performance & Reliability Monitoring System and a Log Monitoring & Alerting System using Prometheus and Grafana, deepening my skills in Kubernetes, observability, and building fault-tolerant backend infrastructure.

Vision

I aspire to work at the intersection of Security Engineering and Site Reliability Engineering (SRE). My goal is to join a team building secure, observable systems that serve millions and can withstand and recover from failures efficiently.
